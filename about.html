Karura Community Chapel is a non-denominational congregation started in January 1996 as a plant of the Nairobi Chapel.

our journey
our vision

Becoming Christ-like Disciples Engaging the World.

Becoming

We, none of us, have arrived, but all are called to the process. We are all Committing to continual growth through C-H-R-I-S-T.

C-entred on God
H-oly in life
R-ighteous in action
I-ntegrated in the Body
S-pirit filled for Service
T-otally engaged

our mission To be:

A worshiping community, helping one another,

To be:

Obedient to God’s Word,

To be:

Ambassadors of Jesus Christ in a needy world.

core values

Love
A community growing in our connection with God. (Centred on God; Holy in life; Righteous in action; Integrated in the Body (of Christ); Spirit-filled for service; and Totally Engaged as followers of Christ in all aspects of their lives). (John 15:4-5)
Prayer
We believe that successful ministry is dependent on God, and so foundational to the above is a Commitment to Prayer, the truth of God’s Word, and seeking God’s presence and enablement by the Holy Spirit. (John 14 – 17; Rom. 8:5-17; Phil. 2:13).
Leadership
Commitment to empowering leadership (Ephesians. 4:11,12) (open, unthreatened, team-oriented, unity seeking, willing to pass on to others)
Relevance
Commitment to Contextual Relevance (1 Cor. 9:19 to 23).
Church Planting
Commitment to Church Planting (Matt. 28:19,20)
Transformation
Commitment to Biblical transformation (Rom. 12:1,2; Col. 1:28,29)

our leadership

At the heart of our church is a dedicated team of leaders who are passionate about guiding our community in faith, service, and growth. Committed to living out our values and vision, they inspire and support us on our spiritual journey, helping to shape a stronger, more connected church family
